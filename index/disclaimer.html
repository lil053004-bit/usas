2. Substantial Risk of Loss

WARNING: Trading and investing involves substantial risk. Key risks include:

Market Risk

Stock prices can be highly volatile and may fluctuate significantly due to market conditions, economic factors, company performance, political events, and other factors beyond anyone's control. You could lose a substantial portion or all of your investment.

Volatility Risk

Securities markets can experience extreme price volatility. Rapid price changes can result in significant losses in a short period of time. High volatility increases the risk of substantial losses.

Liquidity Risk

Some securities may be difficult to buy or sell quickly. Limited liquidity can result in significant losses if you cannot exit a position at your desired price.

Leverage Risk

Using leverage (borrowed money) magnifies both gains and losses. You can lose more than your initial investment when using leverage. Leverage substantially increases risk.

Company-Specific Risk

Individual companies may face operational challenges, management issues, competitive pressures, regulatory problems, or bankruptcy. Company-specific events can cause rapid and severe price declines.
